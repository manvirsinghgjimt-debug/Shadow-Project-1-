How to Write a Mini Project Report for MCA

A well-written project report is as important as the code itself in MCA assessments. It demonstrates that a student understands what they built, why they made certain decisions, and what they learned from the experience. A strong report combines technical accuracy with clear and readable writing.

The recommended structure for an MCA shadow project report includes a title page, problem statement, project objective, dataset or corpus design, baseline method, improved method, testing and observations, comparison table, AI tools used and what was learned, and a conclusion. Each section should be concise and evidence-based, supported by screenshots and code snippets where relevant.

Students at GJIMT use Notion AI to organize and summarize their findings, and Grammarly AI to improve grammar and clarity in the final document. However, the raw content and technical analysis must come from the student's own observations during testing. AI tools are used only to polish and structure, not to fabricate analysis.
