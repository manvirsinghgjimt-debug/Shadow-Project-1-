Viva Preparation Tips for MCA Shadow Projects

The viva is the final and most important stage of the MCA shadow project assessment. It tests whether students genuinely understand what they built, or whether they simply submitted borrowed code and AI-generated content. Performing well in the viva requires honest engagement with the project throughout all six weeks, not last-minute revision.

Common viva questions for the Mini Google Search Engine shadow project include: What is the difference between searching and ranking? Why did you give higher weight to title matches than content matches? What was your baseline approach and how did you improve it? Which AI tool helped you most and for what purpose? What would happen to your program if the number of pages grew to 10,000?

To prepare effectively, students should re-read their own code and be able to explain every function and variable. They should practice tracing through the scoring logic step by step for a sample query. Students can also ask ChatGPT to generate five viva questions based on their project and then answer those questions aloud to build confidence and identify gaps in their understanding.
